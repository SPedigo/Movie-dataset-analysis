Introduction¶

The dataset I selected for this project is the TMDB dataset. It gives the name, budget, revenue, cast, website and more for some movies released in the box office that year.Years range from 1960 to 2015. The question I am analyzing the data to answer is what are the common characteristics of successful movies? For this project, I will measure success in two ways:by a movie's voting average and by its vote count. These are my dependent variables. My independent variables are runtime, year, and budget. I used these to create three questions to explore. 1. Does a movie's runtime affect whether it will be successful? 2. Does a movie's budget affect whether it will be successful? 3. What years have the most successful movies?

Conclusions¶

I analyed this data to look for trends that may describe what traits make a movie successful. To measure success, we looked at vote counts and vote averages. Runtime seems to affect a movie's success. Those that did well stayed under 200 minutes. The effects of budget on success was inconclusive. The higher the budget the higher the vote count, in general. However, a higher budget is associated with a lower vote average. Further testing needs to be done. Year does seem to affect success. The most successful movies are ones that were made in the 2000's up to 2015. This could be for any combination of the following reasons: more movies are made now than decades before, graphics are better now than ever before, and internet access/the ability to rate movies did not come around until recent years. This research does not show strong correlations or conclude causality. It focuses on observable trends and simple statistics.

Limitations¶

One limitation of this project is that the data is not all-inclusive. Many recent movies are included but the data becomes more spaced out the further back in time you go. Another limitation is that movies that came out more than 20 years ago came out before internet was in everyone's homes. This means that the ability to survey opinion or have knowledge of the budget of a film was much harder. This dataset could be separated into two chunks (before and after internet became available) for further analysis. The final limitation I will look at is the gaps in information. Many movies are missing budget and revenue values. These could be used to look for stronger trends.
